The outlook wasn’t brilliant for the rebel nine that day;
The score stood four to two, with but one inning left to play.
Then when Michael popped out Dagon, and fanned Moloch to his shame, 
A sickly silence fell upon the side that forced the game.

A straggling few got up to go in deep despair. The rest
Clung to that spite which springs eternal in the jealous breast;
They thought if only Satan could but get a whack at that—
We’d put up even money now with Satan at the bat.

But Baal preceded Satan, as did also Beelzebub,
And the former was a bullock, and the latter was a grub;
So upon that stricken multitude grim melancholy sat,
For there seemed but little chance of Satan's getting to the bat.

But Baal deflected low, and charging first beat out a ground 
And Beelzebub's reply, a shot, chased Michael from the mound;
And when the dust had lifted, and all saw what had occurred,
There was Beelzebub at second - and Baal besmirching third.

Then from 5,000 throats and more there rose a lusty yell;
It rumbled through the heavens, and it rattled down in hell;
It knocked upon the mountain and recoiled upon the flat,
For Satan, mighty Satan, was advancing to the bat.

There was ease in Satan's manner as he stepped into his place;
There was pride in Satan's bearing and a smile on Satan's face.
And when, responding to the cheers, he lightly doffed his hat,
No stranger in the crowd could doubt ’twas Satan at the bat.

Ten thousand eyes were on him when he raised his bat in hate;
Five thousand tongues applauded when he pointed t'ward the Gates.
And as Metatron stepped to the mound, with God's Words on his lips,
Defiance gleamed in Satan's eye - and doom in Satan's grip.

From Heaven's Mouth a Holy Word was forming in the air,
And Satan stood a-watching it in haughty grandeur there.
Straight at the sturdy rebel a Word of God was sped—
“That ain’t the word,” said Satan. “Strike one,” Raguel said.

From the benches, dark with devils, there went up a muffled roar,
Like the beating of the storm-waves on a stern and distant shore.
“Kill him! Kill the umpire!” shouted some one on the stand;
And they would have killed Raguel had not Satan raised his hand.

With a smile of baleful charity black Satan's visage shone;
He stilled the rising tumult and he bade the game go on;
He beckoned to great Metatron, who cast a Word anew;
But Satan still ignored it, and Raguel said, “Strike two.”

“Fraud!” cried the maddened thousands, and the echo answered fraud;
But one scornful look from Satan and the audience was awed.
They saw his face grow stern and cold, they saw his muscles strain,
And they knew that Satan wouldn’t let the Word go by again.

The sneer is gone from Satan's lip, his teeth are clenched in hate;
He holds his wrath in silence as impatiently he waits. 
And Metatron now forms a Word, and now he lets it go,
And now the air is shattered by the force of Satan's blow.

Oh, above in favored Heavens now, the light is shining bright;
The cherubim are playing, and the angels' hearts are light,
And somewhere souls are laughing, and somewhere angels shout;
But there is no joy in hell -- for mighty Satan has struck out.
